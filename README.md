# Data-scraping-policies
Data scraping policies from 48 digital platforms operated by S&amp;P 500 companies

## Overview

This dataset provides a curated collection of data scraping, crawling, and harvesting policies from 48 digital platforms operated by S&P 500 companies. It is designed to support research in areas such as platform governance, data access regulation, web scraping ethics, and digital policy analysis.

The dataset was manually compiled by extracting relevant policy sections from the most recent publicly available terms of service, acceptable use policies, or related documentation of each platform. Platforms were selected from S&P 500 companies to ensure coverage of large, influential digital ecosystems. No platform-specific inclusion criteria were imposed beyond this scope, allowing for a broadly representative and generalizable sample.

## Data Schema

- Platform Name: Official name of the digital platform whose data scraping policy is analyzed.
- Company Ticker: Stock ticker symbol of the S&P 500 company that owns or operates the digital platform.
Platform Type: Type of platform, which is exclusive and selected from a predefined list (Social Network, Communication Service, Search Engine, Digital Media Platform, Location-based Services, Developer Infrastructure, Marketplace, Hospitality Platform, Consumer Intelligence, Business Intelligence).
- Base URL: The primary homepage URL of the platform.
- Policy URL: Direct URL to the platform’s terms of service, acceptable use policy, or scraping-related policy page.
- Text: Raw, unprocessed textual content of the scraping-related policy, extracted from the policy page.

## Coverage

- Temporal Coverage Date: 02/08/2026

- Location: California, United States


## Collection Methodology

The dataset was manually constructed by extracting sections related to data scraping, crawling, and harvesting from the most recent publicly available terms and conditions of selected social media platforms. All platforms in the dataset are operated by S&P 500 companies. The selection was based on their status as leading firms, without applying platform-specific inclusion criteria, in order to preserve randomness and support the generalizability of the analysis.
